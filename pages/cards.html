Cirta:
A Canvas Where Civilizations Pulse Through Its Rocks** Cirta, the "City of Hanging Bridges," shines like a gem that harbors within its cliffs and valleys a rare human legacy. Every suspended bridge carries a tale, and every stone whispers the history of nations that once thrived: from the Romans in Tiddis, to the Ottomans in Ahmed Bey Palace, all the way to the Islamic architecture of Emir Abdelkader Mosque. Here, you traverse time as you wander through its ancient markets, steadfast museums, and neighborhoods steeped in the fragrance of centuries. Cirta is not merely a city of bridges—it is an open-air museum where history grazes the sky, and culture flows like a river, weaving through civilizations cradled by the earth and the sun.

The Rock of Constantine was the cradle of one of the oldest cities in the world, the ancient Cirta, capital of Numidia, and was the scene of a succession of civilizations thereafter. This imposing mass which supports the city rises to 664m at its highest point, surrounded "by the Rhumel gorges with a length of 1800m and a depth of 35m at the beginning, at Sidi Rached, the height of the cliffs then increases rapidly to reach at the exit of the gorges nearly 200m".
